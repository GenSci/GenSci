Coming Soon! Gentlemen Scientists.

Gentlemen Scientists aims to be a place of scientific collaboration and dissemination with a focus on marine science. We seek to share tools and data as well as methods and results.

What is a Gentleman Scientist?

An independent scientist (historically also known as gentleman scientist) is a financially independent scientist who pursues scientific study without direct affiliation to a public institution such as a university or government-run research and development body. The expression "gentleman scientist" arose in post-Renaissance Europe but became less common in the 20th century as government and private funding increased.

Most independent scientists have at some point in their career been affiliated with some academic institution, such as Charles Darwin who was affiliated with the Geological Society of London.

Wikipedia

* The term Gentlemen Scientist is used to evoke a romantic, old-fashioned notion of an independent scientist such as Charles Darwin or Nikola Tesla. It is not intended to suggest that only male scientists can participate. Here at GenSci we welcome scientists and collaborators of all genders.

Purpose

The purpose of Gentlemen Scientists is to disseminate scientific discoveries and ongoing research in marine and coastal sciences to the wider community. Additionally it seeks to facilitate collaboration and communication between scientists utilizing some of the tools and methodologies from the world of software development.

Goals

The goals of Gentlmen Scientsts are many and growing as needs or new use cases are identified. Some identified goals are as follows:

  • A blog in which scientific discoveries can be documented and research can be shared.
  • A set of code repositories to share any custom solutions developed to aid in research endeavors.
  • A collaboration interface to allow groups of researchers to discuss their work, share analyes, and draft public releases of research.
  • An application interface that allows registered scientists to store data in a geographic context and view publicly available data that may interact with their study domain.
  • A community discussion forum where scientists and the greater public can actively communicate about past, ongoing, and future research.

Methods

Gentlemen Scientists is intended as a marriage between web application development and oceanographic study of remote sensing data. The site is developed using a Python 3 framework (Django) and thus much of the initial code base will likely be using that language.

Where possible, graphics will be interactive and directly interpret actual data, which will be available for download.

The D3 visualization framework will be employed as well as some Python based graphing libraries.

Next Steps

FIrst project: Physical Factors Influencing Phytoplankto Abundance In Southern Monterey Bay

The likely first project to be developed on GenSci will simply be the master thesis of C. Ryan Manzer, the founder of GenSci. The intent is to present the scientific research, develop interactive visualizations, share analytical code (in both MatLab and Python), and link to data sources.

This research can provide a jumping off point for future collaborative work.
